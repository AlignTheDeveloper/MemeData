GIMM 260 Data Narrative

What topic is your narrative about?

For my narrative, I decided to collect data about the general consensus that the GIMM community has reached concerning the nature of the GIMM 260 Meme Channel and community. I chose this topic because I am one of the main contributers to the GIMM 260 Meme Channel and I was interested in what the rest of our major's opinion on the channel was. To collect the data for my narrative I constructed a questionaire that asked for some basic information from the person filling it out. Then, I asked Jeanne to send the questionaire out in an email and on the GIMM discord. I also posted a link to the questionaire in every discord/discord channel related to GIMM and memes to get as many responses as I could. By the end of it, I received 52 responses and had oodles of data to sift through.

Were there any specific questions you wanted to answer?

Yes actually! When I created my google forms questionaire, I made a "crossroads question". That question was:

Have you heard about the GIMM260 Jack Memes?

From this question, depending on the answer, the person taking the questionaire would be directed to one of two different sections.

If the responder answered no,

They would be directed to an "Out of the loop" section where I asked them for more information to see why they haven't heard about the memes and gave them the option to view the memes if they wanted to. (I gave them a link to the discord hehe)

If the responder answered yes,

They were directed to a "Meme Connoisseur" section where I asked them if:

  • They were in the GIMM 260 Discord yet
  • They had ever shared or forwarded the memes
  • Which meme style they thought was most prominent
  • Which meme style they liked the most
  • And some questions about their views on the nature of these memes

What conclusions can be made based on the data you collected?

Based on the provided data from the survey about GIMM260 Jack Memes, several conclusions can be drawn:

Awareness and Engagement:

There is a notable awareness of the GIMM260 Jack Memes among the students, particularly within the 7th and 8th cohorts (which account for 62.7% of the respondents). This suggests that the memes could be a recognizable part of the student experience in this program. Of those that filled out the questionnaire, 37/52 (71.2%) had heard about the memes.

Perception of Community and Culture:

Of the 71.2% of responders who had heard of the memes, 30/37 (81.1%) believe that they well represent the culture of GIMM and contribute to a sense of unity. This indicates that the memes could be playing a positive role in building community within the program.

Impact on Program Image:

Of those who responded, no one thought that the memes were crossing a line and 35/37 (94.6%) of them answered that the memes were in good humor. This positive reception suggests that the memes are contributing favorably to the program's image, at least among those who are aware of them.

Subject's Perception:

Many students believe that Professor Polifka is amused by the memes, suggesting that they are seen as a respectful and light-hearted addition to the community.

Meme Styles and Preferences:

The most commonly encountered style of meme is "Jack's face on a famous character," (25/37 or 67.6% of those that had heard of the memes) It is also one of the styles found most amusing.

The exact percentages were:
  1. Jack's face as a texture on a 3D modeled sphere - 13/37 or 35.1%
  2. Jacks face on a famous character - 12/37 or 32.4%
  3. Jack's face in a popular meme format - 9/37 or 24.3%
  4. Steel Blue related “other” responses - 2/37 or 5.4%
  5. Both Popular meme and 3D sphere “other” responses - 1/37 or 2.7% (they couldn’t choose lol)

This preference for a specific style could guide the creation of future memes to better align with popular taste.

Diversity in Engagement:

While most of the respondents have heard of these memes, 15/52 (28.8%) of them had not. This could indicate an opportunity to broaden the reach of these memes within the community or to explore different platforms or methods for sharing them.

Professor Engagement:

The data includes 2 responses from a professor, though one of the “professors” also said they were from the ninth cohort so I am speculative of the authenticity of their response. Also, (unless it was a poser) Professor Polifka himself was one of the respondents! I won't out him too much, but for those that are wondering, his favorite memes were the ones of him on a famous character and he believes the memes are in good humor!

Feedback and Further Engagement:

Of those who had not heard of the memes, 13/15 (86.7%) expressed interest in viewing the memes to provide further feedback, indicating an active interest in this aspect of the community culture.

DISCLAIMER

It's important to note that these conclusions are drawn from a limited dataset and might not fully represent the entire GIMM community. Additionally, the data is self-reported and subjective, which can introduce biases. A more comprehensive analysis, possibly including a larger sample size or additional data points, could provide deeper insights.
